<h2 id="overview">Overview</h2>

Planning a budget trip to Kerala is easier than you think. By leveraging local transport and choosing affordable homestays, you can explore the best of God's Own Country for less than ₹2,000 a day. Here are five of the best budget itineraries for different types of travelers.

<h2 id="itinerary">Top Budget Itineraries</h2>

### 1. The Classic Backpacker Route (4 Days / ₹6,000)
**Route:** Kochi -> Alleppey -> Varkala
This is the ultimate introduction to Kerala on a shoestring.
- **Day 1 (Kochi):** Take the Water Metro to Fort Kochi. Stay in a budget hostel (₹400). Walk around the Chinese fishing nets and Jew Town. Eat local biryani at Kayees (₹150).
- **Day 2 (Alleppey):** Take the local train from Ernakulam to Alleppey (₹50). Instead of a houseboat, take the SWTD government ferry to See Kuttanad (₹40 round trip). Stay in a homestay near the canals (₹600).
- **Day 3 (Varkala):** Take a train to Varkala (₹80). Spend the evening at the North Cliff. Surf or relax on the beach. Stay at a cliff-side hostel (₹500).
- **Day 4 (Varkala):** Visit the Janardanaswamy Temple and Ponnumthuruthu Island before heading to Trivandrum for your departure.

### 2. The Misty Hills Escape (3 Days / ₹4,500)
**Route:** Kochi -> Munnar -> Kochi
Perfect for escaping the heat.
- **Day 1 (Munnar):** Take a KSRTC bus from Ernakulam to Munnar (₹150). The 4-hour ride is incredibly scenic. Stay in a budget dorm or homestay in Old Munnar (₹500).
- **Day 2 (Munnar):** Rent a scooter (₹500/day) and visit Mattupetty Dam, Echo Point, and Top Station. Eat local meals at Thattukadas (₹300 for the day).
- **Day 3 (Munnar to Kochi):** Visit the Tea Museum (₹120) before catching the afternoon bus back to Kochi.

### 3. The Northern Untouched Beauty (3 Days / ₹4,000)
**Route:** Kozhikode -> Wayanad
For those who want to avoid the typical tourist crowds.
- **Day 1 (Kozhikode):** Explore SM Street and eat the famous Kozhikode Halwa and Biryani at Paragon or Rahmath (₹200). Spend the evening at Kozhikode Beach.
- **Day 2 (Wayanad):** Take the stunning Thamarassery Churam bus ride to Kalpetta (₹80). Stay in a backpacker hostel (₹400). Visit Pookode Lake or hike Chembra Peak.
- **Day 3 (Wayanad):** Visit the Edakkal Caves (₹40) and explore the tea estates of Vythiri.

<h2 id="budget-breakdown">Budget Breakdown</h2>

No matter which itinerary you choose, your daily costs will look roughly like this:
- **Stay:** ₹400 - ₹800 (Hostels or Budget Homestays)
- **Food:** ₹300 - ₹500 (Local Bakeries, Thattukadas, Meals on Banana Leaf)
- **Transport:** ₹100 - ₹300 (KSRTC Buses, Local Trains, Auto-rickshaws for short distances)
- **Activities:** ₹100 - ₹300 (Most temples, beaches, and hikes are free or very cheap)

<h2 id="tips">Pro Tips</h2>

- **Book Trains in Advance:** Even sleeper class tickets sell out fast. Book your local train tickets on the IRCTC app at least a week prior.
- **Eat Like a Local:** Ask for "Meals" or "Oonu" at lunchtime. It’s unlimited rice with multiple curries for just ₹80 - ₹100!
- **Carry Cash:** While UPI is widely accepted, small tea stalls in remote hill stations might only accept cash.
