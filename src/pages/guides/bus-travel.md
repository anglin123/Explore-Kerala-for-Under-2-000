<h2 id="overview">Overview</h2>

The Kerala State Road Transport Corporation (KSRTC) is the lifeline of the state. These iconic red and yellow buses navigate everything from multi-lane highways to incredibly steep, winding mountain roads in Munnar and Wayanad. For a budget traveler, mastering the KSRTC network is the key to exploring Kerala for under ₹2,000 a day.

<h2 id="types-of-buses">Types of KSRTC Buses</h2>

Understanding the different types of buses will save you time and money:

1. **Ordinary (Red and Yellow):** The cheapest option. These stop at every single bus stop. Great for short distances (e.g., within a district), but agonizingly slow for long journeys.
2. **Fast Passenger (FP):** Slightly more expensive than Ordinary, but they only stop at major junctions. The sweet spot for budget travelers.
3. **Super Fast (SF) & Super Express:** Faster, with fewer stops. Ideal for traveling between districts (e.g., Ernakulam to Trivandrum).
4. **Minnal:** The fastest non-AC buses that only stop at major district headquarters. Highly recommended for long distances.
5. **Low Floor AC (Volvo) & Swift:** The premium AC services. These are more expensive but highly comfortable. A great budget alternative to taking a taxi from the airport!

<h2 id="budget-breakdown">Fares & Budget Estimates</h2>

Bus travel in Kerala is incredibly cheap. Here are some estimated fares for popular routes (Super Fast/Fast Passenger):
- **Ernakulam (Kochi) to Munnar (4 hours):** ₹130 - ₹160
- **Trivandrum to Varkala (1.5 hours):** ₹50 - ₹70
- **Ernakulam to Alleppey (1.5 hours):** ₹60 - ₹80
- **Kozhikode to Wayanad (3 hours):** ₹90 - ₹110

<h2 id="tips">Pro Tips for Bus Travel</h2>

### 1. Boarding and Alighting
Buses in Kerala stop very briefly. You must be quick. Enter through the rear door and exit through the front door (unless it's a Low Floor Volvo, where doors are automatic). 

### 2. The Language Barrier
Destination boards are usually written entirely in Malayalam. Don't panic! The KSRTC conductors and locals are extremely helpful. Just shout your destination (e.g., "Munnar?") to the conductor before boarding, and they will wave you in or point you to the right bus.

### 3. Women's Safety
Kerala buses are generally very safe for women. The front row of seats (behind the driver) on all KSRTC ordinary and fast passenger buses are strictly reserved for women. 

### 4. Motion Sickness
The mountain roads to Idukki (Munnar) and Wayanad (Thamarassery Churam) have dozens of hairpin bends. If you are prone to motion sickness, take medication beforehand and try to get a seat near the front of the bus.

<h2 id="itinerary">How to Find Routes</h2>

While Google Maps transit directions are improving in Kerala, the most reliable way to find bus timings is:
1. **The 'Aanavandi' App:** A third-party app maintained by enthusiasts that lists KSRTC timings.
2. **En Aanavandi Website:** Great for checking schedules.
3. **Just Ask:** The inquiry counters at major KSRTC bus stands (like Ernakulam or Trivandrum Thampanoor) are very reliable.
